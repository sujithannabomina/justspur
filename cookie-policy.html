Cookie Policy

Cookies are small files that help websites work better and remember your preferences. Here's exactly what we use, why, and how you can control it.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about you — like that you're logged in, or your preferences — so you don't have to re-enter it every time.

There are different types of cookies: some are essential for a site to function at all, while others are optional and used for things like analytics or advertising. We'll explain exactly which ones we use and why.

Simple version: Cookies help JustSpur keep you logged in and work properly. We use very few of them — and zero advertising cookies.

2. Our Approach

JustSpur uses a minimal cookie approach. We believe in your right to privacy, so we only use cookies that are genuinely necessary or that provide a meaningful benefit to your experience.

We do not use:

  • Advertising or targeting cookies of any kind
  • Cookies to track you across other websites
  • Third-party social media tracking pixels
  • Any cookies to build a profile for selling to advertisers

JustSpur is an ad-free platform. This means we have no commercial reason to track you beyond what's needed to make the platform function.

3. Types We Use

Essential Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are required for JustSpur to function. Without them, you cannot log in, your session would not persist, and the app would not work. You cannot opt out of these without stopping using the service.

Functional Preference Cookies

These remember your preferences and settings — for example, your chosen date categories, notification preferences, and display settings. They make your experience more personalised and consistent.

Analytics Performance Cookies

We use basic, privacy-respecting analytics (Google Analytics with IP anonymisation) to understand how the platform is being used — which features are popular, where users drop off, and how to improve the experience. No personal data is linked to these analytics.

You can opt out of analytics cookies without affecting your use of JustSpur.

5. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are set by third-party services we use to operate JustSpur:

Google Firebase

Firebase handles our authentication and real-time database. It sets session cookies necessary for secure login. Google's cookie policy applies: policies.google.com/privacy

Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics with full IP anonymisation enabled. Analytics data is aggregated — we cannot identify individual users from it. You can opt out using Google's opt-out browser extension.

Razorpay

Razorpay sets a temporary cookie during payment processing for fraud detection. This cookie is cleared after your payment session. Razorpay's policy: razorpay.com/privacy

6. Your Choices

Browser settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block all cookies, block only third-party cookies, or delete existing cookies. Here's how in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Safari (iPhone/iPad): Settings → Safari → Privacy & Security
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Samsung Internet: Settings → Privacy → Cookie Preferences

What happens if you block cookies

Blocking essential cookies will prevent you from logging in to JustSpur and using the platform. Blocking analytics cookies has no effect on your ability to use JustSpur.

Opt out of analytics

To opt out of Google Analytics specifically without affecting anything else, install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

7. Do Not Track

Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal to websites. JustSpur respects DNT signals — when we detect a DNT signal, we disable all non-essential cookies (analytics and functional) for your session.

Essential cookies required for login and security are still used regardless of DNT status, as without them the platform cannot function at all.

8. Changes to This Policy

If we introduce new cookies or change how existing ones are used, we will update this policy and the "Last Updated" date. For significant changes that affect your privacy, we will notify you by email.

We commit to keeping our cookie usage minimal and transparent as the platform grows.
